Mehndi Design and Mehndi Different Culture
and Origin

We are all aware of the importance of Mehndi. It is regarded vital and auspicious not just during wedding ceremonies,
but also throughout many religious festivals such as Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha, and others. But did you know that, in
addition to the traditional version, Mehndi is also applied in a variety of other styles?.
Allow us to introduce you to a variety of popular mehndi designs from around the world.


1. Pakistani Mehndi Design

Pakistani Mehndi is more about Pakistani cultures and ideals than it is about Indian Mehndi, which is understandable.
While many other elements, such as paisleys and floral artwork, stay the same, Pakistani mehndi incorporates mandalas,
domes, a greater number of leaves, and, in many cases, mosque doors. This style is characterized by stronger outlines
and elaborate artwork.


2. Indian Mehndi Design

Indian mehndi is the most popular and widely seen in India, and it consists of everything that is uniquely Indian!
Indian mehndi style has always had something special to offer for every age and every event, from single trails on the back and
front of the hands to full hand coverage on both sides. While bridal mehndi adorns the hands with dholkis, palkis, bride-
groom artwork, mandap, kalash, and other auspicious artwork symbolizing a wedding ceremony, non-bridal mehndi has
a lot to offer as well.

For obvious reasons, non-bridal design is more popular, and they come in a variety of styles, ranging from a single
'bel'/trail to a one-sided design, light mehndi artwork to incredibly heavy full hand mehndi. Paisleys, florals, designs,
dots, concealed names, and items of traditional significance are all part of the Indian mehndi style.
3. Indo-Arabic Mehndi Design

This mehndi design is a great combination of Indian and Arabic styles. Consider the beauty and complexity of Indian mehndi designs, which are separated by bold lines and empty spaces. It's one of the most gorgeous pieces of fusion art I've ever seen. This mehndi design style incorporates all of the traditional Indian mehndi designs, such as leaves, florals, paisleys, and chequered designs, but within the boundaries of finely made bold lines that are separated from one another in such a way that it looks exquisitely eye-catching!

4. Arabic Mehndi Design
It's a popular mehndi art style that's noted for its unusual aesthetic, which includes bold lines and empty spaces. The name comes from the fact that this design is thought to have originated in the Gulf countries. It's quite different from Indian mehndi, and it exudes a certain sense of majesty and grace. Unlike Indian mehndi, which has a lot of detail, Arabic mehndi is all about simple but bold designs. The empty space in this form of mehndi is quite important, since it draws attention to the neighboring designs/designs. Arabic mehndi is now one of the most widely used mehndi styles around the world.

5. Moroccan Mehndi Design
Moroccan mehndi, which is popular among both genders, is noted for its unisex designs and is also used by males who are interested in henna tattoos. Geometric shapes and curves, traditional tribal designs, diamond shapes, and prominent dots are all part of this mehndi style. It has its own charm and can readily be distinguished from ordinary mehndi designs as being unique and unusual.

6. Western/Contemporary Mehndi Design
The wild west world has also taken to this fad, which is more commonly referred to as a 'henna tattoo' than mehndi. This sort of mehndi design has little to do with tradition, and the mehndi is applied solely for the 'temporary-tattoo' effect. Geometrical forms, swirls, eccentric shapes, and trendy motifs are common designs in this style. The large gaps between motifs are usually created in such a way that the pattern resembles hand-worn ornaments.

7. Indo-Western Mehndi design
Indo-Western, like Indo-Arabic, combines the finest of both worlds! Indo-Western mehndi combines traditional Indian mehndi designs with unconventional designs. Apart from the wacky designs, the most obvious aspect of this form of mehndi art is the addition of small messages.

8. African Mehndi Design
That's right, you read that correctly. Mehndi/Henna has been a part of West African culture for about a thousand years, which is a lesser-known fact. Although it shares many similarities with Arabic mehndi in terms of gaps and harsh edges, African mehndi primarily consists of tribal designs and patterns.
